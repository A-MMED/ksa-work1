Foundations of the Kingdom

The roots of a great nation

📅

When Was the First Saudi State Founded?

The First Saudi State was established in 1139 AH / 1727 AD in the city of Diriyah, located in the heart of the Arabian Peninsula in the Najd region. This historic event marked the beginning of a continuous political and civilizational legacy that spans nearly three centuries.

👑

Who Is the Founder?

The founder of the First Saudi State is Imam Muhammad bin Saud Al Muqrin (died 1765 AD). He was the Emir of Diriyah who, on February 22, 1727, assumed leadership and forged a historic alliance with the Islamic scholar Sheikh Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab, combining political authority with religious reform to build a strong and unified state.

✨

Core Founding Values

Islamic Faith

The state was founded on the principles of Islam, with the Holy Quran and the Sunnah as its constitution.

Justice & Governance

Upholding justice among all citizens and establishing fair governance as a cornerstone of the state.

Unity & Solidarity

Unifying the tribes and regions of the Arabian Peninsula under one banner and one leadership.

Security & Stability

Providing safety and security for all citizens as a fundamental duty of the state.

National Identity

What unites us as one people

“

National identity is the sense of belonging to a homeland — a shared set of values, history, language, religion, and culture that defines who we are as a people and binds us together across generations.

▶

Islamic Religion

Islam is the foundation of Saudi national identity. The Kingdom serves as the guardian of the Two Holy Mosques and upholds Islamic values in all aspects of public and private life.

🗣

Arabic Language

Arabic is the official language of the Kingdom and a pillar of national identity. It is the language of the Holy Quran and a vessel of Saudi culture, literature, and heritage.

🏛

History & Heritage

Nearly three centuries of continuous statehood, from the First Saudi State in Diriyah to the modern Kingdom, form a rich historical tapestry that every Saudi citizen shares.

🏭

Customs & Traditions

Saudi customs — from hospitality (diyafa) and generosity to traditional dress, poetry, and folk arts — are living expressions of a rich cultural identity passed down through generations.

🌍

Land & Geography

The vast Arabian Peninsula — with its deserts, mountains, coasts, and oases — is the physical homeland that anchors Saudi identity to a specific place and landscape.

📚

Vision 2030 & Future

Vision 2030 adds a forward-looking dimension — a shared aspiration for a diversified, innovative, and globally engaged Saudi Arabia.

Founding Day

Celebrating the birth of a nation

22 February 1727
22 Rajab 1139 AH
Annual Public Holiday

What Does This Day Represent?

Founding Day (يوم التأسيس) commemorates the historic moment on February 22, 1727, when Imam Muhammad bin Saud assumed the rulership of Diriyah, marking the official birth of the First Saudi State. This day was officially designated as a national holiday by Royal Decree in 2022, recognizing nearly 300 years of continuous Saudi statehood.

How Do Citizens Celebrate?

🏛️

Historical exhibitions and tours of Diriyah and At-Turaif UNESCO heritage site

🎭

Cultural performances, traditional arts, poetry, and folk music events

📚

Educational programs in schools highlighting the history of the Saudi state

🎆

Fireworks displays, light shows, and public festivities across the Kingdom

🟢

Wearing the national color green and displaying the Saudi flag on homes and vehicles

What Event Does It Commemorate?

Saudi National Day commemorates the historic proclamation made on September 23, 1932, by King Abdulaziz bin Abdul Rahman Al Saud (Ibn Saud), who officially unified the Kingdom of Nejd and Hejaz and renamed it the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.

🎉

Aspects of Celebration

🎇

Grand fireworks displays in major cities

🟢

Citizens dress in green and decorate homes and streets

🎤

Concerts, theatrical performances, and national song events

✈️

Air shows by the Saudi Hawks aerobatic team

🍽️

Traditional food festivals and cultural markets

Flag Day

The symbol of sovereignty and faith

11 March

Celebrated annually every March 11

What Does the Saudi Flag Symbolize?

Green Color

Represents Islam and the prosperity of the land.

⚔
The Sword

Symbolizes justice, strength, and the readiness to defend the nation.

☺
The Shahada

The Islamic declaration of faith inscribed on the flag.

★
White Color

Represents purity, peace, and the clarity of the Islamic message.

Why Was a Day Dedicated to the Flag?

Saudi Flag Day, observed on March 11 each year, was established to honor the national flag as a supreme symbol of Saudi sovereignty, unity, and identity. The day serves to deepen citizens' pride in their flag, educate new generations about its symbolism, and reinforce the values of loyalty and belonging to the homeland.

How Is Flag Day Celebrated?

🚩

Raising the Saudi flag on all government buildings and public spaces

📖

Educational activities about the history and symbolism of the flag

🎨

Art competitions and creative projects for students

📱

Social media campaigns expressing national pride
